# Invest-Ed Privacy Notice

**PRIVACY_NOTICE_VERSION = 2026-05-16**

When you sign up to Invest-Ed, we show you this notice and record that you (or your parent or guardian) agreed to it. If we make important changes, we will show you the updated notice and ask you to agree again before you can keep using the app.

This notice is written for young learners aged 8 and above. If there is anything you do not understand, please ask a grown-up to help you.

---

## What is Invest-Ed?

Invest-Ed is a learning app that teaches you about money — how to save it, how to spend it wisely, and how the world of investing works. To do that, we need to know a few things about you so that the app works properly and keeps you safe.

---

## What information do we collect?

When you join, we collect:

- **Your username** — the name you use to log in. It does not have to be your real name.
- **Your date of birth** — so we know your age. We use this to make sure younger learners get the right level of protection (for example, involving a parent or guardian).
- **Your country** — so we know which rules apply to your data and which currency to show you.
- **A password** — we store this in a scrambled form so no one can read it, not even us.

We also collect:

- **Your email address** — only if you are old enough to have one under the rules in your country. Younger learners do not need to give an email.
- **A parent or guardian's email address** — if you are below the age of digital consent in your country, we send your parent or guardian an email asking for their permission before you can use the app.
- **Your learning progress** — things like which lessons you have completed, how many experience points (XP) you have earned, your level, and your streak count. This helps the app remember where you left up and show you what to do next.
- **Which topics you are learning about** — for example, saving or investing. We use this to show you the right lessons.

We do **not** collect:
- Your home address or phone number.
- Your photo or any images.
- Information about your family's finances.

---

## Why do we collect it?

| Information | Why we need it |
|---|---|
| Username and password | So you can log in safely |
| Date of birth | To check your age and keep you safe |
| Country | To follow the right rules for your country |
| Parent or guardian email | To get their permission if you are young |
| Learning progress | So the app remembers what you have done |
| Topic choice | To show you the right lessons |
| Your email (if given) | To send you account messages |

We only use your information to run the app and keep it safe. We do not use it to sell you things or to follow you around the internet.

---

## Who can see your information?

- **You** — you can always see your own information inside the app.
- **Your parent or guardian** — if they gave permission for your account, they can see your progress and ask for a copy of your data.
- **The Invest-Ed team** — to keep the app running, fix problems, and make sure it is safe. The team is not allowed to share your information with anyone else.

We do **not** share your personal information with advertisers, data brokers, or any other company.

---

## Lesson videos (YouTube)

Some lessons include educational videos embedded from YouTube. When a video loads, YouTube (a Google service) may receive technical data — such as device and IP information — so it can play the video. We use YouTube's privacy-enhanced mode (`youtube-nocookie.com`), which does not set tracking cookies unless a video is actually played. We do **not** share your child's account details with YouTube. Google handles this technical data under its own privacy policy.

---

## How we measure what works (product analytics)

To make InvestiKid better, we count how features are used — for example how many
children finish a lesson, or tap the "Continue" button on the home screen. These
counts are collected **by us only** (no third-party analytics companies, no
advertising trackers) and contain no names, messages, or anything your child
types. While an account is open, the counts are linked only by an internal
account number so we can see patterns like "do children come back after a week?";
they are **never** used to build a profile of your child or to change what your
child sees. If the account is deleted, that link is removed, and in any case we
delete the raw counting records after 13 months.

---

## How long do we keep your information?

We keep your information for as long as you have an account with us.

If you or your parent or guardian asks us to delete your account:

1. We close the account straight away. You will not be able to log in.
2. After 30 days, we delete most of your personal information from our systems. We keep your year of birth and country for a short while longer to help us check that we handled your account the right way — but without your name or email, this information cannot identify you.
3. We keep a record of when you joined and when you asked us to delete your account, because the law requires us to show we handled things properly.

---

## Your choices

You are in control of some things on Invest-Ed:

- **Personalised recommendations** — we can suggest lessons based on what you have already done. This is switched **off** by default. You (or your parent or guardian) can switch it on in your account settings if you would like it.
- **Marketing messages** — we do not send marketing emails unless you ask us to. This is switched **off** by default. You can change this in account settings.
- **Face ID / biometric sign-in** — on a phone or tablet, you can choose to unlock the app and sign in with Face ID, Touch ID, or your device's biometric unlock instead of typing your password. This is **off** by default and is optional. For children, a parent or guardian turns it on first. Your face or fingerprint is checked **on your device by your phone** — we never see or receive any biometric data; we only store a random sign-in key in your device's secure storage, which you can remove at any time by switching this off.
- **Delete your account** — you or your parent or guardian can ask us to delete your account at any time. We will do this as described above.
- **See your data** — you can ask for a copy of all the information we hold about you. For younger learners, a parent or guardian can do this on your behalf.
- **Fix your information** — if something is wrong, you can update it in your profile settings.

**A note for younger learners:** if you are below the age limit in your country, your parent or guardian manages these choices for you. That is not to stop you having a say — it is to make sure a grown-up is looking out for you.
