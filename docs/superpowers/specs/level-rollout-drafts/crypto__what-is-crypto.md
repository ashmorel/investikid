### Level 2 (free) — How it works & how to stay safe

**Theme:** the next layer beyond L1 — how a blockchain keeps itself honest without a bank, what a wallet and a private key really are, and the warning signs of crypto scams.

#### Card 1 — How does a blockchain stay honest? (xp 10)
**A chain that's hard to cheat**
A blockchain stores transactions in "blocks" that are joined in order, like links in a chain. Each new block carries a fingerprint of the one before it, so if anyone tried to secretly change an old block, the fingerprints would stop matching and the whole network would notice. Thousands of computers keep their own copy and compare notes, so there's no single record a cheat could quietly rewrite. That's the clever part: trust comes from lots of computers agreeing, not from one boss in charge.

#### Card 2 — Your keys, your responsibility (xp 10)
**Wallets, keys, and "not your keys, not your coins"**
A crypto wallet doesn't really "hold" coins — it holds keys. Your *public* key is like an account number you can share so people can send to you. Your *private* key is the secret password that lets you spend, and it must NEVER be shared. Here's the scary bit grown-ups need to know: if you lose your private key, there's no "forgot password" button and no bank to ring — the crypto is usually gone forever. And if a scammer gets your private key or "seed phrase", they can take everything in seconds.

#### Quiz 1 (xp 25)
**Q: Why is it so hard to secretly change an old transaction on a blockchain?**
- Because a security guard checks every block
- ✅ Because each block carries a fingerprint of the one before it, and thousands of computers compare copies
- Because the government keeps the only copy
- Because old blocks are printed out on paper
**Explanation:** Each block is linked to the previous one by a fingerprint, and thousands of computers hold matching copies — so a secret change would break the chain and everyone would spot it.

#### Quiz 2 (xp 25)
**Q: Which key should you NEVER share with anyone?**
- Your public key (like an account number)
- ✅ Your private key or seed phrase (the secret that lets you spend)
- The name of the coin
- The price you paid
**Explanation:** Your public key is fine to share so people can send to you. Your private key (or seed phrase) is the secret that controls everything — sharing it is like handing someone the keys to your whole wallet.

#### Quiz 3 (xp 25)
**Q: A website promises "Send 1 coin and we'll send you 2 coins back — guaranteed, today only!" What is this most likely?**
- A normal, safe way to grow money
- ✅ A scam — "double your money, guaranteed, act now" is a classic crypto trick
- A government savings scheme
- A bank offering interest
**Explanation:** Guaranteed doubling, "today only" pressure, and "send first" are textbook scam signs. Real investing is never guaranteed, and nobody legit asks you to send crypto to get more back.

#### Quiz 4 (xp 25)
**Q: You forget the private key to your crypto wallet. What usually happens?**
- You ring a helpline and they reset it
- The bank refunds you
- ✅ There is often no way to recover it, and the crypto can be lost for good
- It automatically emails you a new key
**Explanation:** Unlike a bank account, most crypto has no "forgot password" and no customer-service reset. Lose the key and the crypto is usually gone — which is one reason it's risky.

#### Scenario (xp 20)
**Prompt:** You're playing an online game and someone you've never met messages: "I'll give you free crypto — just send me your wallet's secret recovery phrase so I can add it." What do you do?
- **Refuse and tell a trusted grown-up** → *Exactly right. Your secret recovery phrase controls everything in the wallet. Nobody who is genuine ever needs it — anyone asking is trying to steal from you. Telling a trusted adult is the safe move.*
- **Send it — free crypto sounds great** → *This is a trap. Handing over your recovery phrase lets a scammer empty the wallet instantly. "Free crypto" used as bait is one of the most common online scams.*
- **Send just half of the phrase to be safe** → *Still unsafe — you should never share any part of a secret recovery phrase, and a stranger asking for it at all is a red flag. Stop and tell a trusted grown-up.*

*Correct choice: refuse and tell a trusted grown-up (index 0).*

### Level 3 (premium) — Thinking critically about crypto

**Theme:** mastery and judgement — how crypto compares to ordinary money, why rules and protections matter, the energy/environment angle, and how to keep a cool head around hype. Advanced, but always cautious.

#### Card 1 — Crypto vs the money in your pocket (xp 10)
**What makes "money" actually money?**
Normal money like the pound does three jobs well: you can spend it almost anywhere, its value stays fairly steady day to day, and the Bank of England helps manage it. Most crypto struggles with those jobs — few shops accept it, its price can swing wildly in a single day, and no central body steadies it. That doesn't make crypto "fake", but it's why many experts treat it more like a risky bet than like everyday money. Understanding the difference helps you see through people who call it "the future of all money".

#### Card 2 — Rules, protection, and the energy question (xp 10)
**Why rules and energy both matter**
If a UK bank fails, the FSCS protects your savings up to £85,000 — but crypto usually has no such safety net, so if a platform collapses or gets hacked, your money may simply be gone. Regulators like the FCA also warn that crypto is high-risk and largely unprotected. There's an environment angle too: some cryptocurrencies (like Bitcoin) use enormous amounts of electricity to run, which has a real climate cost. A thoughtful investor weighs *all* of this — safety, rules, and impact — not just the price.

#### Quiz 1 (xp 25)
**Q: Which is a job that ordinary money (like the pound) does much better than most crypto?**
- ✅ Keeping a fairly steady value you can spend almost anywhere
- Doubling in value every week
- Being impossible to ever lose
- Earning guaranteed profits
**Explanation:** A currency works best when its value is steady and widely accepted. Most crypto swings too much and is accepted in too few places to do that job well — which is a key difference from everyday money.

#### Quiz 2 (xp 25)
**Q: If a crypto platform gets hacked and your crypto is stolen, what protection usually applies?**
- The FSCS refunds you up to £85,000
- The government replaces it
- ✅ Usually none — crypto generally isn't covered by the protections that guard bank savings
- Your school insurance covers it
**Explanation:** FSCS protection covers savings in regulated UK banks, not most crypto. That missing safety net is a big reason regulators call crypto high-risk.

#### Quiz 3 (xp 25)
**Q: A famous person posts "Buy this new coin NOW before you miss out!" What's the smartest reaction?**
- Buy immediately so you don't miss out
- Copy them — famous people can't be wrong
- ✅ Pause, be sceptical of hype and "fear of missing out", and talk to a trusted grown-up before doing anything
- Borrow money to buy more
**Explanation:** "Buy now or miss out" is designed to rush you. Famous people are sometimes paid to promote coins. Slowing down, questioning the hype, and asking a trusted adult protects you from FOMO-driven mistakes.

#### Quiz 4 (xp 25)
**Q: Why do some people raise concerns about the environmental impact of certain cryptocurrencies?**
- They use too much paper
- ✅ Some use huge amounts of electricity to run, which has a climate cost
- They are made of plastic
- They require lots of water to print
**Explanation:** Cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin can use enormous amounts of electricity. That energy use is a real-world cost a thoughtful person factors in, alongside the financial risks.

#### Scenario (xp 20)
**Prompt:** Your older cousin is excited: "I'm putting ALL my birthday money into one new coin a YouTuber promoted — it's going to the moon!" They ask what you think. What's the wisest thing to say?
- **"Sounds amazing — put mine in too!"** → *Risky. Following hype from a video and betting everything on one volatile coin is exactly how people lose money fast. "Going to the moon" is a promise nobody can actually make.*
- **"Maybe slow down — that's a lot of risk on one hyped coin. Let's read about it and talk to a trusted grown-up first."** → *Brilliant. You're spotting hype, the danger of putting everything in one place, and the value of caution and adult guidance. That's exactly how a careful thinker responds.*
- **"Borrow more so you can buy even more!"** → *Never a good idea. Borrowing to buy something this volatile can leave you owing money AND losing the investment. This is one of the most dangerous moves in investing.*

*Correct choice: encourage slowing down, scepticism, and asking a trusted grown-up (index 1).*
